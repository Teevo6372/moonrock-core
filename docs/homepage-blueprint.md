# Section 01 — Homepage Architecture

## Purpose

Define the overall structure of the Moonrock Marketing homepage and establish the customer journey from the first visit through engagement, assessment, and long-term partnership.

The homepage exists to guide visitors toward the right next step based on where they are in their business journey.

---

## Business Objective

Transform the homepage from a marketplace-first experience into a customer-first experience that:

- Speaks directly to startups and small businesses.
- Builds trust before presenting solutions.
- Introduces the Moonrock Flight Plan™.
- Encourages visitors to begin a Launch Assessment, Growth Assessment, or conversation with Nova.
- Supports the Growth Hub without allowing it to become the homepage focus.

---

## Emotional Objective

Visitors should quickly understand:

- Moonrock understands my challenges.
- I don't have to figure everything out alone.
- There is a clear path forward.
- I have options without feeling pressured.

The homepage should replace uncertainty with confidence.

---

## Homepage Structure

The homepage follows this sequence:

1. Hero
2. Recognition
3. Imagine What's Possible
4. Moonrock Flight Plan™
5. Guidance Before Guesswork
6. Meet Nova
7. Growth Hub
8. Final Call-to-Action
9. Footer

Each section prepares the visitor for the next step in the journey.

---

## Customer Journeys

The homepage supports three primary visitor paths.

### Launch Journey

Audience:

Startups and new business owners.

Primary Destination:

Moonrock Launch Assessment

---

### Growth Journey

Audience:

Established businesses seeking sustainable growth.

Primary Destination:

Moonrock Growth Assessment

---

### Guidance Journey

Audience:

Visitors who are unsure where to begin.

Primary Destination:

Nova, Moonrock's Virtual Growth Advisor.

---

## Core Principles

The homepage should always:

- Guide before selling.
- Educate before recommending.
- Build trust before requesting commitment.
- Focus on business outcomes rather than technology.
- Position Moonrock as a Business Growth Partner.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

✓ Homepage structure supports all three customer journeys.

✓ Every section has a single, clear purpose.

✓ The customer journey flows naturally from top to bottom.

✓ Visitors always understand their next available step.

✓ Business outcomes remain the primary focus throughout the homepage.
---

# Section 02 — Design System

## Purpose

Define the visual, interaction, accessibility, and responsive design requirements for the Moonrock Marketing homepage.

The design system must preserve the existing Moonrock logo and color palette while repositioning the website from a marketplace-first experience into a premium Business Growth Partner platform.

The design must support the approved messaging, customer journeys, Moonrock Flight Plan™, Nova, and Growth Hub without interfering with existing WooCommerce functionality.

---

## Emotional Design Objectives

The homepage must create the following emotional progression:

1. Relief
2. Recognition
3. Hope
4. Confidence
5. Trust
6. Support
7. Momentum

Visitors should feel understood before they are asked to take action.

The visual design must support the copy rather than compete with it.

---

## Visual Personality

The Moonrock homepage must feel:

- Premium
- Modern
- Calm
- Confident
- Intelligent
- Approachable
- Forward-looking
- Futuristic without becoming distracting

The homepage must not feel:

- Loud
- Overly sales-focused
- Corporate
- Cluttered
- Generic
- Overly technical
- Gimmicky
- Like an ecommerce marketplace homepage

---

## Existing Brand Requirements

The homepage must preserve:

- The existing Moonrock Marketing logo
- The existing Moonrock color palette
- The XStore Child theme
- Existing WooCommerce product functionality
- Existing checkout functionality
- Existing digital product delivery

The homepage redesign must not require replacing the active WordPress theme.

The homepage may be rebuilt through Elementor while XStore continues handling WooCommerce functionality.

---

## Color Usage

Continue using the existing Moonrock color palette.

The homepage should combine dark premium backgrounds with controlled neon accent colors.

Accent colors may be used for:

- Primary buttons
- Active navigation states
- Icons
- Links
- Progress indicators
- Hover states
- Moonrock Flight Plan™ visual elements
- Nova highlights

Accent colors should not dominate large areas of the page.

Large blocks of body text should not use neon colors.

---

## Backgrounds

Recommended background treatments include:

- Dark primary backgrounds
- Subtle gradients
- Controlled glow effects
- Light atmospheric textures
- Clear contrast between sections

Space-inspired backgrounds may be used when they remain subtle and readable.

Avoid:

- Busy galaxy imagery
- Large planets
- Rockets
- Astronaut stock images
- Visual effects that reduce readability

---

## Typography Standards

### Headings

Headings must be:

- Bold
- Clear
- Benefit-driven
- Easy to scan
- Written in plain language

Only one H1 heading may appear on the homepage.

Section titles should use H2 headings.

Card titles should use H3 headings.

### Body Copy

Body copy must be:

- Conversational
- Easy to understand
- Short enough to scan
- Written directly to the business owner
- Free from unnecessary marketing or technical jargon

Paragraphs should remain short.

Avoid large blocks of uninterrupted text.

### Minimum Readability

Desktop and mobile body text should generally be no smaller than 16px.

Buttons must remain readable without zooming.

Line height must provide comfortable spacing.

Text width should be controlled to prevent overly long lines.

---

## Spacing Standards

White space is an intentional part of the Moonrock design.

Every section must have enough vertical and horizontal space to remain readable and focused.

Sections must not feel compressed.

Cards must not contain competing messages.

Each major section should include:

- Clear top padding
- Clear bottom padding
- Consistent maximum content width
- Visual separation from adjacent sections

---

## Section Rhythm

Every major homepage section should follow this structure when applicable:

1. Section headline
2. Supporting copy
3. Primary content
4. Transition statement
5. Clear next action

No section should introduce multiple unrelated objectives.

Each section must prepare the visitor for the section that follows.

---

## Content Width

The homepage should use a consistent boxed content width.

Recommended maximum content width:

1200px

Full-width background sections may be used, but the primary content inside those sections should remain constrained.

Long-form copy should use a narrower reading width than card grids.

---

## Card Standards

Cards may represent:

- Customer journeys
- Business challenges
- Desired outcomes
- Flight Plan stages
- Brand pillars
- Nova capabilities
- Growth Hub categories
- Final CTA options

Cards must include:

- Rounded corners
- Consistent internal padding
- Clear visual hierarchy
- One primary idea
- A concise heading
- Short supporting copy
- No more than one primary action

Cards must not feel overcrowded.

Interactive cards should:

- Elevate slightly on hover
- Use a subtle border or glow change
- Maintain readable contrast
- Support keyboard focus
- Avoid dramatic motion

---

## Button Standards

Buttons must communicate progress rather than pressure.

Approved button language includes:

- Start My Launch
- Start My Growth Plan
- Build My Flight Plan
- Chat with Nova
- Talk with Nova
- Explore the Growth Hub
- Ask Nova for a Recommendation

Avoid:

- Book a Demo
- Contact Sales
- Schedule a Consultation
- Buy Now as the dominant homepage CTA

Primary buttons should use the strongest approved accent treatment.

Secondary buttons should use an outline or lower-emphasis treatment.

Buttons must:

- Use descriptive labels
- Include hover and focus states
- Meet contrast requirements
- Be large enough for touch interaction
- Avoid relying only on icons

Recommended minimum touch target:

44px by 44px

---

## Icon Standards

Use one consistent icon family throughout the homepage.

Preferred style:

Clean line icons

Icons should:

- Communicate meaning quickly
- Support headings and cards
- Use accent colors sparingly
- Remain visually consistent

Avoid mixing unrelated icon styles.

Avoid using emojis as the final production icon system unless intentionally approved during implementation.

---

## Animation Standards

Animation must improve clarity and reinforce movement.

Approved animation types:

- Soft fade-in
- Gentle upward movement
- Subtle hover elevation
- Smooth section transitions
- Controlled progress animations
- Subtle glow changes

Avoid:

- Flashing effects
- Constant pulsing across multiple elements
- Spinning elements
- Large parallax movement
- Autoplay video with sound
- Animation that delays access to content

Animations must respect reduced-motion accessibility preferences.

---

## Moonrock Flight Plan™ Presentation

The Moonrock Flight Plan™ should be treated as a signature visual experience.

The five stages are:

1. Discover
2. Navigate
3. Launch
4. Accelerate
5. Horizon

The Flight Plan may use:

- Connected lines
- Subtle orbital paths
- Progress indicators
- Stage icons
- Scroll-based highlighting

The Flight Plan must remain understandable without animation.

The visual treatment should feel elegant and premium rather than playful or cartoon-like.

---

## Nova Presentation Standards

Nova must be presented as:

Moonrock's Virtual Growth Advisor

Nova must appear:

- Calm
- Confident
- Welcoming
- Professional
- Intelligent
- Approachable

Nova must not appear:

- Cartoonish
- Exaggerated
- Robotic
- Aggressive
- Overly sales-focused

Nova should be presented as a digital member of the Moonrock team rather than a generic chatbot.

---

## Growth Hub Presentation

The Growth Hub must not resemble the current marketplace-first homepage.

WooCommerce products should be positioned as practical resources.

The homepage may display:

- Growth Hub categories
- Nova's Picks
- Featured resources
- Short product outcome descriptions

The homepage should not emphasize:

- Large discount banners
- Product grids above the primary customer journey
- Marketplace-first messaging
- Price-first presentation

---

## Mobile Design Requirements

Mobile is a primary experience.

Requirements include:

- Vertical stacking
- Clear content order
- Large touch targets
- Readable typography
- Reduced animation
- Fast loading
- Consistent spacing
- Clear CTA hierarchy
- No horizontal scrolling

Three-column desktop layouts should stack into one-column mobile layouts unless testing supports another structure.

---

## Accessibility Requirements

The homepage must meet WCAG AA accessibility principles where practical.

Requirements include:

- Sufficient text contrast
- Keyboard navigation
- Visible focus states
- Descriptive link text
- Descriptive image alt text
- Proper heading order
- Accessible button states
- Reduced-motion support
- No information communicated by color alone

Accessibility is a launch requirement.

---

## Performance Requirements

The homepage must prioritize mobile performance.

Requirements include:

- Optimized image sizes
- Modern image formats when supported
- Limited animation libraries
- Limited autoplay content
- Lazy loading for below-the-fold imagery
- Minimal unnecessary scripts
- Removal of unused homepage widgets
- Avoidance of oversized video files

Nova video content should not block initial page loading.

---

## Elementor Requirements

The homepage should be built using Elementor containers where possible.

Requirements:

- Use consistent global spacing
- Use global typography where practical
- Use global colors where practical
- Avoid excessive nested containers
- Avoid duplicated styling
- Assign clear container names
- Keep WooCommerce templates separate from homepage content
- Do not modify XStore core files
- Preserve the XStore Child theme

Reusable components should be saved when they will appear on multiple pages.

---

## WooCommerce Protection Requirements

The redesign must not disrupt:

- Existing product URLs
- Existing product files
- Existing order records
- Existing customer accounts
- Existing checkout settings
- Existing digital download permissions
- Existing payment processing

WooCommerce changes must be tested separately from homepage design changes.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

✓ The existing Moonrock logo and palette remain recognizable.

✓ The homepage no longer feels marketplace-first.

✓ Startups and small businesses can identify their path quickly.

✓ Every section has one clear purpose.

✓ Accent colors guide attention without overwhelming the page.

✓ Cards and buttons remain consistent.

✓ Nova feels like a trusted Moonrock team member.

✓ The Moonrock Flight Plan™ feels like a signature framework.

✓ The Growth Hub supports the customer journey.

✓ Mobile visitors receive a complete experience.

✓ Existing WooCommerce functionality remains unaffected.

✓ The homepage meets accessibility and performance requirements.
